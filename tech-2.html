Color-Theory

Color theory plays a huge role in photography yet not many photographers have a good foundation knowledge
or know how to utilize it to their benefit.

Colors that contain physical pigment, such as in the case of paint, are called subtractive color.
This is because pigmented objects absorb light or subtract color wavelength from the visible light spectrum.
The unabsorbed wavelength is reflected off the object and seen by the human eye as color.

We can't control the colors in the scene during shooting.
But we can certainly enhance or reduce the effect of color globally or selectively in post-processing.
